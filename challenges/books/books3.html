The War of Art


  • "Late at night have you experienced a vision of the person you might become, the work you could acomplish, the realized being you were meant to be? Are you a writer who doen't write, a painter who doen't paint, an enterpreneur who never starts a venture? Then you know what Resistance is"

  • " Hitler wanted to be an artist. At eighteen he moved to Vienna to live and study. He applied to the Academy of Fine Arts and later to the School of Architecture. Ever see one of his paintings? Resistance beat him. It was easier for Hitler to start WWII than to face a blank square of canvas."

  • "Resistance's goal is not to wound or disable. Resistance aims to kill. Its target is the epicenter of our being: our genious, our soul, the unique and priceless gift we were put on earth to give and that no one else has but us. Resisrance means business. When we fight it we are in a war to the death."

  • "Procrastination is the most common manifestation of Resistance because it's the easiest to rationalize. We don't tell ourselves, "I'm never going to write my symphony." Instead we say, "I'm going to write my symphony; I'm just going to start tomorrow.""
